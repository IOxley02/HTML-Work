My Mission

From the beginning, my mission has been to be proud of my profession and ensure my work is outstanding. I am confident in my current skillset and look foward to showcasing my knowledge in the future. While studying with ASU, I've taken up personal projects to exercise my skills and challenge myself to learn something new.

While working, I developed a belief in perfection. I program on the basis that the user comes first: How I can make their experience easy, appealing, and with 100% accuracy. I seek opportinities which I can bring my best and be apart of something impacting everyday lives.

Why Computer Science?

I grew up on computers, spending my time browsing websites, watching youtube, pretending I'm an artist, or most of all playing video games. When I was young, the internet always fascinated me, I could play my games with anyone, meeting people who lived in different states, or even coutries. Also the sheer magnitude of content I had available to me. Games, education, social media, you name it, the internet has it all at the tap of a screen. But, when I was older, I realized Computer Science is a lot of math - So what kept me here? Well thinking back to art, I realized that programming is just a means for non-artistic people to create something utilizing mathematics. Artists have their five phases: inspiration, percolation, preparation, creation, and reflection, and I believe programming also has five phases. starting with coming up with the idea, makeing diagrams, determining what environment to use, codeing, and finishing off with debugging - pretty similar if you ask me. Also, both have that same satisfaction when it all comes together, producing a great end result and considering "done". Well maybe not done since programs always have room for improvement. Anyways, programming is a means for me to create and that's what I like about it. The thought of users visiting my website, or using my programs, that's what keeps me here.
